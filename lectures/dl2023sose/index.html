Deep Learning: Architectures & Methods

Lecture: Tuesdays, 11:40 - 13:20 o'clock, L101
Exercises: Wednesdays, 11:40 - 13:20 o'clock, S202/C205 - Bosch Hörsaal
Language: English

Goal. Artificial Intelligence has become ubiquitous in our society, with applications in search, image understanding, apps, mapping, medicine, drones, and self-driving cars. Recent developments in neural network (aka “deep learning”) approaches have greatly advanced the performance of these state-of-the-art AI resp. machine learning systems. This online lecture collection is a deep dive into details of the deep learning architectures with a focus on learning end-to-end models, particularly for image classification. Your will also get in touch with how to implement, train and debug your own neural networks and gain a detailed understanding of main approaches within the research on deep learning.


Lectures


We will mainly follow an existing online course due to Fei-Fei Li (Stanford): CS231n: Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition. That is you will watch the next video lecture prior to our lecture, prepare a list of 5 questions as main homework for the video you have watched, and then we will try to answer your question in class as a group together. Additionally, we will also use the existing online course due to Sergey Levine's (Berkeley) CSW182/282A: Designing, Visualizing and Understanding Deep Neural Networks and the slides of its previous editions due to John Canny (UC Berkeley). They cover similar topics. We will also present our own stuff, sometimes with videos from previous editions. We may even ask you to read papers. Next to the “5-questions” homework, there are also more traditional exercises, and you can propose and run a deep learning project.

This is the syllabus based on previous iterations of the course. The * marks the main video we expect you to watch.
