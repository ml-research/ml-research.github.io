Nexplore

AICO — AI in Construction

A Collaboration Lab of the TU Darmstadt and Nexplore

Nexplore was founded by HOCHTIEF in 2018 as a Group-wide platform for digital innovation and cooperates with scientists and leading universities worldwide.

Mission

Close integration of research in information technology, Artificial Intelligence, and the construction industry.

For example, we develop conversational AI, probabilistic databases, and learning robots for the construction sector.

Research Vision

Cutting-Edge AI to Empower Construction

We seem to live in the golden age of Artificial Intelligence (AI). Sustained algorithmic advances coupled with the availability of massive datasets and fast parallel computing have led to breakthroughs in applications that would have been considered science fiction even a few years ago. Over the past five years, voice-controlled personal assistants have become commonplace, image recognition systems have reached human performance in certain tasks, and autonomous vehicles are rapidly moving toward reality. Consequently, there is no doubt that machine learning and modern AI will transform many areas of our economy and society.

The construction industry, however, seems to lag behind in implementing innovative solutions using artificial intelligence. AICO wants to change this. How can AI help improving processes such as design optimization, scheduling, documentation, safety and construction project monitoring. How can large amounts of data be made accessible to human intuition? How can man and machine together solve complex design tasks? How can machines help us to react to unforeseen changes to the project plan? Disciplines that used to focus on reducing complexity through codes and symbols (e.g. 2D drawing in architecture, etc.) are now facing the challenge, but also the enormous opportunity to work differently and better thanks to large amounts of information. The key to this is not only the data itself, but rather the joint discovery and understanding of patterns and meaning that are hidden in it. Can AI be combined with Intelligence Amplification (IA) - the enhancement of human intelligence through digital systems - to form a single entity?

In short, artificial intelligence still poses a major challenge in the construction industry - for both sides, humans and machines. To close this gap, the Collaboration Lab "AI for Construction" - AICO for short - has set itself the goal of bringing the research areas of information technology - especially AI and computational cognitive science - and the construction industry closer together.

Projects

We develop deep and probabilistic construction and building information modeling databases, robot learning methods, and conversational AI for the construction sector.

Dual PhD Program in AI

Because AI provides solutions for many challenges, knowledge transfer is essential in the field of AI. We have to build up AI expertise in the companies themselves. Only then, companies will be able to use and develop new AI technologies.

The nucleus of AI knowledge transfer is the personnel transfer of well-trained, constantly learning domain and AI experts who are able to continuously develop new solutions in the industry and keep pace with global innovations. Thus, AI knowledge transfer must take place in particular via "people."

To this end, AICO established one of the first dual PhD programs in AI. It combines regular internships at HochTief with collaborative PhD research at the TU Darmstadt. We believe that this is strong instrument for building a dynamic economy of ideas.

Thanks to the strategic investment of the State of Hesse into the Hessian Center for Artificial Intelligence, linking the state’s universities via 20 additional AI faculty, headquartered in Darmstadt, AICO offers access to leading AI experts. It also connects to the ELLIS Unit Darmstadt, a team of world-class faculty and fellows, who are leaders across a broad range of AI — from machine learning to natural language processing, from computer vision to robotics — as well as in AI’s twin, computational cognitive science.
