ELLIS Unit Darmstadt on Intelligent Systems

Mission

Developing intelligent systems that understand context and meaning — that can adapt flexibly to novel scenarios — much like us humans.

For example, we develop deep and probabilistic models and reinforcement learning methods for machines and humans. Together with our Centre for Cognitive Science, we emphasize the interface between human and machine intelligence.

Research Vision

Human-like Intelligent Systems

We seem to live in the golden age of machine learning (ML). Sustained algorithmic advances coupled with the availability of massive datasets and fast parallel computing have led to breakthroughs in applications that would have been considered science fiction even a few years ago. Over the past five years, voice-controlled personal assistants have become commonplace, image recognition systems have reached human performance in certain tasks, and autonomous vehicles are rapidly moving toward reality. Consequently, there is no doubt that ML and modern AI will transform many areas of our economy and society.

However, current intelligent systems, while impressive, are still rather “narrow”. They are highly specialized systems that are very good at specific, well-defined tasks. Consider selfdriving cars. As stunning as they are, they utilize a composite of narrow intelligent (sub-) systems. If you put that same software into a golf cart, it is likely to be useless without reprogramming and -training. By contrast, any human who had learned to drive a car could get into a golf cart for the first time and have no major problem navigating the fairways. This is because humans are very good at abstraction: we can easily generalize solutions and apply them to similar but different problems. To overcome such limitations and reach their true potential, intelligent systems must become more human-like.

Research

We develop deep and probabilistic models, and reinforcement learning methods for machines and humans. Together with our Centre for Cognitive Science, we emphasize the interface between human and machine intelligence.

Strong AI Expertise Right in the Heart of Europe

The strength of the ELLIS Unit Darmstadt is its team of world-class faculty and fellows, who are leaders across a broad range of artificial intelligence — from machine learning to natural language processing, from computer vision to robotics — as well as in AI’s twin, computational cognitive science.

Thanks to the strategic investment of the State of Hesse into the Hessian Center for Artificial Intelligence, linking the state’s universities via 20 additional AI faculty, headquartered in Darmstadt, our Unit offers access to industry partners, on-campus labs, and startup support. We not only help shaping ELLIS through participating in and leading various ELLIS programs, but TU Darmstadt also contributes its experience as coordinator of UNITE!, a network of Europe’s leading universities of technology. Darmstadt, as a city of science, is a highly dynamic and international place. It has been leading Germany’s “City of the Future” ranking for the past five years. It is embedded in the Frankfurt-Rhine-Main metropolitan area, a major hub with a multitude of companies and institutions — right in the heart of Europe.
